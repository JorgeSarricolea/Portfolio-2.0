Body Mass Index (BMI) is a measure of the relationship between a person's weight and height. It is calculated by dividing a person's weight in kilograms by their height in meters squared. BMI is a common way to assess whether a person has a healthy weight, as it can be an indicator of a person's body fat.


  • 0 - 16: Very low weight
  • 16 - 18.5: Low weight
  • 18.5 - 25: Normal range (healthy)
  • 25-30: Overweight
  • 30 - 35: Obese Class I - Moderate obesity
  • 35 - 40: Obese Class II - Severe obesity
  • 40 and above: Obese Class III: Very severe obesity

    Basal Metabolic Rate (daily): kcal

    Basal Metabolic Rate (BMR) is the amount of energy the body burns at rest to maintain basic bodily functions such as breathing, blood circulation, and body temperature. BMR is expressed in calories per day.

    For men: BMR = (10 x weight in kg) + (6.25 x height in cm) - (5 x age in years) + 5

    For women: BMR = (9.6 x weight in kg) + (1.8 x height in cm) - (4.7 x age in years) + 655

    Calories to Add or Remove: kcal

    This is an approximate value of calories that you should add or subtract depending on the goal, whether it is to gain or lose weight. This value is calculated by multiplying the necessary calories of the Basal Metabolic Rate by 0.2.

    Grams of protein intake: gr

    The general recommendation for protein intake is around 0.8 grams per kilogram of body weight per day for healthy adults with sedentary lifestyles. However, for individuals who are more physically active or who are trying to build muscle, protein needs may be higher.

    So, to calculate your protein needs, you would need to multiply your weight in kilograms by the appropriate factor based on your activity level and other individual factors.

  • Little or no exercise (1 to 3 days a week) = 0.8gr per kg
  • Moderate training (3 - 5 days a week) = 1.2gr per kg
  • Intense training (6 - 7 days a week) = 1.6gr per kg
  • Very Intense Training (6 - 7 days a week, 2 times a day) = 2gr per kg
